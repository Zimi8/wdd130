Your Safety is Our Priority

Prepare for adventure. Prepare for the river.

Health and Physical Requirements

All participants must meet certain physical and mental prerequisites to ensure a safe experience for themselves and the group. Rafting requires full physical autonomy and the ability to react quickly to instructions.

  • Full Autonomy: Must have complete control over your body and movements.
  • Impact Resistance: Must be able to withstand minor impacts and sudden movements.
  • Stairs: Must be able to ascend and descend over 300 meters of stairs (access to the river).
  • Climbing: Must be able to climb and re-board the boat with minimal assistance.
  • Comprehension: Must be able to understand and transmit verbal safety instructions from the crew.
  • Respiration: Must be able to maintain stable breathing in the mist of the waterfalls.
  • Load Capacity: Must be able to support a specific load or weight in case of emergency.

Failure to meet these conditions, either permanently or temporarily, may result in the cancellation of your excursion for safety reasons. This is non-negotiable.

Essential Rafting Guidelines

Rafting is an exhilarating sport, but it requires respect for the river and strict adherence to our team's guidance. Follow these simple rules to ensure maximum fun and safety.

DO's (The Essentials)

  • Listen to the Guide: Your guide is an expert; follow all commands immediately.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water regularly before and during the trip.
  • Wear PFD: Always keep your Personal Flotation Device (life vest) properly secured.
  • The T-Position: If you fall out, float on your back with your feet downstream (feet-first) to fend off rocks.
  • Hold the T-Grip: Keep your hands securely on the T-grip of the paddle.

DON'Ts (Avoid These)

  • No Alcohol/Drugs: Do not consume alcohol or non-prescribed drugs before the trip.
  • Don't Stand Up: Never stand up in the raft while moving.
  • No Diving: Never jump into the river without explicit instruction from the guide.
  • Avoid Cotton: Do not wear heavy cotton clothing; it holds water and gets cold.
  • No Loose Items: Leave valuables, electronics, and unsecured glasses on shore.
